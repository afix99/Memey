    a book about her

    Memey

    Everything she is, the way I see her,
    the way I'll always see her.

    I

    chapter one

    How it started

    It started because she's beautiful. That's just the truth.

    I was scrolling one day and saw a photo of her wearing a New Era cap. I wasn't even following her yet. Something about the way she wore it, the confidence in how she carried herself. I followed right after that. I was still figuring out a lot of things back then, still learning what good style even looked like, but I knew that whatever she was doing, it was right. It was effortlessly right.

    After that first follow, I started noticing everything. Every post she put out, I'd stop scrolling for it. I was a silent admirer for over a year. She didn't even know I existed. She had no idea there was someone on the other side of the screen who genuinely looked forward to everything she posted. I'd see her photos and almost feel my heart skip, and then just keep scrolling like nothing happened, because what was I supposed to do.

    For more than a year I watched from a distance. Then one day I said something. And she said something back. That was the beginning of everything.

    I don't think she understands how much that meant to me. Every small thing she did from that point felt like winning something I didn't know I was even competing for.

    The moment everything began

    I woke up one morning and saw her reply in my notifications and I genuinely could not calm down. I typed "APA NI QUADRUPLE WIN" to myself like an idiot. I still stand by that. Every small thing she did felt like winning something I didn't know I was even competing for.

    II

    chapter two

    Her face

    She doesn't think she has "face beauty." She actually believes this. She has said it more than once, casually, like it's a fact she settled with herself a long time ago. I never know whether to laugh or to be genuinely upset that she sees herself that way.

    Her face has this perfect alignment. Pipi and dagu sitting exactly right. Not symmetrical in a fake way, but naturally balanced. There's a softness to her features that you can't engineer. You can tell she was a soft-faced child and she grew into something quietly stunning without ever losing that warmth.

    When she blushes, it's real. Pink rises in her cheeks without warning, without her permission, and it gives her away before her words do. When she laughs, her whole face gets involved. Her pipi cramps from it. She doesn't laugh halfway. She laughs fully, completely, with her entire expression.

    She is bareface in some photos and still the most beautiful thing on my screen. Cantiknya even bareface. There's nothing performed about the way she looks. She doesn't need anything extra. The beauty is just there, quiet and consistent, whether she's dressed up or not, whether she knows I'm looking or not.

    She doesn't see it. That's the saddest part. She genuinely doesn't see what I see when I look at her.

    III

    chapter three

    Her style

    She never misses. I don't mean occasionally. I mean never, not once.

    Every outfit is intentional. Every post is a statement. She has this rare thing where she doesn't look like she's following trends, she looks like she invented them and the world caught up later. The leather jacket that matched her bag so perfectly it looked accidental until you realised it definitely wasn't. The way she pairs colors. The way she owns her presence in whatever she's wearing.

    You cannot fake that. That kind of style comes from actually knowing who you are, and she clearly does. It's not about following what's trending. It's about knowing what works for her, owning it completely, and making even the simplest choices look like they belong in a magazine.

    Fashion to her isn't vanity. It's a language she speaks fluently. It's the way she shows up to the world. Every choice is deliberate, every pairing thought through. And she does it so effortlessly that people assume it's easy. It's not easy. It just looks easy because she's that good at it.

    That kind of confidence and taste is genuinely unmatched. She makes other people look like they're trying. She isn't trying. She just is.

    IV

    chapter four

    Her mind

    She told me her own principle once. She said she didn't think she had face beauty growing up, so she decided she was going to make sure there was beauty in her brain instead. She said it casually, like it was just a small thing. But when I heard it, I realised that was everything about her in one sentence.

    She's brilliant. Not in a performative way. Not in the way people announce their intelligence at parties. In the quiet, structural way. Where you only notice it after a while, when you realise she's been three steps ahead this whole time.

    Her mind works fast. Hundreds of words come at once, hundreds of emotions, and when she finally types something out, it's already distilled. Already the right thing to say. Sometimes she'll send something and immediately worry it was too much. It never is. What she sees as "too much" is what most people would consider their best thinking.

    She reads philosophy books. Not to impress anyone. She genuinely loves the questions. She thinks about love, about identity, about what it means to live well. She wrestles with ideas the way most people wrestle with their feelings.

    She doesn't just understand what you say. She understands what you mean.

    That is the most rare thing about her mind. Not the intelligence itself. The way she uses it. She listens before she judges. She evaluates people fairly. She is emotionally articulate in a way that takes most people decades to develop. She can name what she's feeling, sit with it honestly, and communicate it without cruelty.

    A moment between words

    She wrote me this whole thing once about the difference between being in love and being well-loved. The difference between something intense and something consistent. I read it twice. Then I wanted to read it a hundred more times.

    V

    chapter five

    Her writing

    She cannot study from messy pages. So she made her notes beautiful.

    She taught herself calligraphy. She learned how to make her writing flow on the page like art. Every assignment, every revision sheet, every lecture summary, all of it composed with care. Most people just write things down. She composes them.

    This is who she is at her core. She doesn't just do things. She does them properly. She respects the act of creating something enough to do it well. Most people would call this perfectionism. I call it discipline. The kind nobody teaches you. The kind you build yourself because you decided your work deserves your full attention.

    She writes poems sometimes. She journals her thoughts at midnight. She has a scrapbook where she presses flowers from cafes and restaurants she visits, tucking them between pages with little notes. She collects small beauties. She moves through the world with attention. She is the kind of person who notices things most people walk past.

    When she sends a long message and then immediately follows it with "sorry, too long," I want to tell her every time. That message was not too long. It was extraordinary. The fact that she apologises for the very thing that makes her remarkable still hurts me sometimes.

    VI

    chapter six

    Her heart

    She calls herself a cry baby. She says it like it's a flaw, like something she should be embarrassed about. But the truth is it's one of the things I love most about her.

    She cries at the right things. She cries because she feels deeply, not because she's weak. She is one of the strongest people I know and she still cries, and those two things live in her at the same time without contradiction.

    She has empathy that's built into how she moves through the world. She feels other people's emotions before they finish their sentences. She worries about strangers. She has been sad over a stray cat she had never even met. A stray cat she passed somewhere. That's how wide her empathy goes.

    Underneath all the composure, the beautiful notes, the fashion, the philosophy, there is a lover girl in there who has kept that part quiet because the world hasn't always been kind to it. She's careful about who she lets in. She takes love seriously. She doesn't do things halfway, and that includes how she feels about people.

    She is brilliant and she's scared of the dark. She is emotionally intelligent and she overthinks everything. She is stunning and she genuinely doesn't believe it. She is put-together and she cries easily. All of it is real at the same time. That contradiction isn't a contradiction at all. It's her whole shape.

    VII

    chapter seven

    Her home

    She has two cats. Latte and Ash. She talks about them like they are characters in her life, with personalities and quirks and small daily dramas. Latte on top. Ash below. Ash once tried to eat her hair. The way she describes them is so warm that you can feel how much she loves them just from her words.

    Her papa calls her Memey too. Her mama is her best friend in the way only some daughters and mothers are. She has this deep, quiet way of caring about her family. She wants to make her parents proud. She already does, every single day, whether she sees it or not.

    She makes coffee for her papa in the middle of conversations. She refills the water jug at night and asks him to come downstairs with her because she doesn't like being alone in the dark. She is grown but still her papa's girl in the sweetest way, and there's nothing childish about it. It's just love that hasn't been worn down by anything.

    The way someone treats their family tells you who they really are. Memey loves hers like it's the most important thing in her world, because to her it is.

    That kind of love at home is where everything else about her comes from. She knows how to give it because she has always been surrounded by it. And she pays it back twice as much.

    VIII

    chapter eight

    Her faith

    Her faith is woven into who she is. Not announced loudly. Not worn as a label. Just present in the small fabric of how she lives.

    She went to an Arabic school for part of her childhood. She fell in love with the way Arabic script moves across a page, and that's part of why she learned calligraphy. She writes Quranic verses sometimes, not as a chore, but because it brings her peace.

    What I love about how she carries her faith is that it isn't performance. She doesn't talk about it constantly or use it to look pious. It just shows up in how she lives. In her honesty. In her belief that what you give out comes back to you. In the small kindnesses she does without telling anyone.

    I have my own way of carrying my faith too, and one of the ways it shows up now is in my prayers. I never miss putting her name in them. Not in a dramatic way. Just consistently, every time. The first prayer of every morning has her name in it. That has become a small constant in my life. A quiet ritual that started without me planning it.

    I pray for her health. I pray for her ease. I pray that whatever is heavy in her week gets lighter. I pray that she gets to keep being herself, fully, without the world ever convincing her she's anything less.

    IX

    chapter nine

    What she wants in love

    She has thought about this more deeply than most people will in their entire lifetime.

    She doesn't want grand gestures. She doesn't want intensity that burns bright and fades. She wants something consistent. Someone who shows up on ordinary days. Someone who checks in when busy. Who stays gentle on her worst days. Who still chooses her when there's nothing exciting to choose.

    She wants to feel safe and still feel wanted at the same time. A love that is both a fireplace and a heartbeat. Calm but not boring. Steady but not empty.

    She has talked about the difference between being in love and being well-loved. The difference between someone wanting the fireworks and someone wanting the quiet morning after. The clarity she has about all of this is something I will never stop being amazed by. She is not naive about love. She has watched enough of it go wrong in other people's lives to know exactly what she does and doesn't want.

    What she wants is real love. The kind that is hard to find because most people don't know how to give it. The kind that requires patience and intention and actual attention to a person.

    She deserves all of it. Every single piece of it.

    And what I want, more than anything I have ever wanted, is to be the one who proves to her that this kind of love exists. That she wasn't asking for too much. That she was right to hold out for it.

    X

    chapter ten

    What makes her rare

    She was homeschooled for part of her life. She started school late, walked into a class where everyone had already formed their circles, and without trying, without forcing anything, everyone wanted to be around her. The older students resented her for it. She had done nothing except be herself.

    That's the exact thing about her. She doesn't perform likability. She just is. There's no act, no carefully managed version of herself she pulls out for new people. The girl her closest friends know is the same girl the world meets in passing.

    She is the same person in the light and in the dark. The same girl who dresses beautifully is the girl who cries at midnight. The same girl who writes essays about philosophy is the girl who sends voice notes when she can't find words. Both versions are her. Neither version is a performance.

    That kind of consistency is one of the rarest things in a person. Most people have a public version of themselves that is curated and a private version that doesn't match. With her, what you see is what you get. She doesn't soften her edges for an audience, and she doesn't sharpen them for one either. She just shows up as herself, every time.

    That's why she's irreplaceable. Not because of any one thing. Because of the way every part of her fits together honestly. The intelligence, the empathy, the style, the faith, the family, the writing, the cry baby softness, the brilliant midnight mind. All of it living together in one person without contradiction.

    XI

    chapter eleven

    What she's done to me

    I've written pages about her. Not because she asked me to. She doesn't even know the half of it.

    I write because when I try to explain what I see in her, words aren't enough and I have to keep trying. I write until 4 in the morning. I set my alarm for subuh and pick up where I left off. I do it because she deserves to have someone try to get it right.

    She made me want to be a better person. Not in a way I said out loud to myself. In a way that just happened, slowly, because knowing her raises the standard. You can't know someone like Memey and stay exactly the same. She changes you. She changed me.

    I never even prayed for someone like her. I didn't think it was possible. Someone like her actually existing, and somehow being in my life. But deep inside, I had been crying for exactly this kind of person to come into my world. And then she did.

    Now my mornings start with her name in my prayers. My evenings end with checking if she's eaten, if she's rested, if her day was okay. The most important things in my day are no longer about me. That's how I know it's real. That's how I know this isn't going away.

    She is the best thing that has happened to me in my entire life. And I will spend as long as it takes making sure she knows it.

    She is the girl with the scrapbook full of pressed flowers,
    the leather jacket that never misses, the calligraphy notes
    and the philosophy books and the two cats she loves completely.

    She is a cry baby and a lover girl and one of the most
    intelligent people I've ever known, all at once.

    She is far more beautiful than she has ever allowed herself to believe.

    And I will spend as long as it takes reminding her of that.

    for memey, always
