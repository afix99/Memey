written by haikal, for her

Memey

Everything she is — the way I see her,
the way I'll always see her.

I — how it started

I was watching her
for almost a year.

I'm not gonna lie — it started because she's beautiful. That's just the truth. I was scrolling one day and she posted a photo wearing a New Era cap. I wasn't even following her yet. Something about the way she wore it, the confidence in how she carried herself — I followed right after that. I was still figuring out a lot of things back then, still learning what good style even looked like, but I knew that whatever she was doing, it was right. It was effortlessly right.

After that first follow, I started noticing everything. Every post she put out, I'd stop scrolling for it. I was a silent admirer for over a year. She didn't even know I existed. She had no idea there was someone on the other side of the screen who genuinely looked forward to everything she posted. I'd see her photos and almost feel my heart skip — cantiknya ya Tuhan — and then just keep scrolling like nothing happened because what was I supposed to do.

507 days after I first followed her, I left a comment. She noticed it. She texted me. "Oh ni ke Afiq?" — that's it, that's all she said. And I don't think she understands how much that one message meant. That was the beginning of everything.

Can you imagine someone you adored for almost a year suddenly starting to text you? It felt unreal. It still feels unreal some days.

— haikal

I woke up one morning and saw her reply in my notifications and I genuinely could not calm down. I typed "APA NI QUADRUPLE WIN" to myself like an idiot. I still stand by that. Every small thing she did felt like winning something I didn't know I was even competing for.

The moment everything began
II — her face

Bareface and
still the most beautiful.

She told me once that she doesn't think she has "face beauty." She actually believes that. She said it like it was a settled fact, like she'd thought about it a long time ago and made peace with it. I didn't know whether to laugh or to be genuinely upset that she sees herself that way.

Her face has this perfect alignment — pipi and dagu sitting exactly right. Not symmetrical in a fake way, but naturally balanced. When she blushes, it's real. Pink rises in her cheeks without warning, without her permission, and it gives her away before her words do. When she laughs, her whole face gets involved. Her pipi cramps from it. She doesn't laugh halfway — she laughs fully, completely, with her entire expression.

She was bareface in one photo and I couldn't stop looking at it. Cantiknya even bareface. I sent that message and I meant every word of it. There's nothing performed about the way she looks. She doesn't need anything extra. The beauty is just there, quiet and consistent, whether she's dressed up or not, whether she knows I'm looking or not.

She doesn't see it. That's the saddest part. She genuinely doesn't see what I see when I look at her.

— haikal

And her fashion. She never misses. I don't mean occasionally — I mean never, not once. Every outfit is intentional. Every post is a statement. She has this rare thing where she doesn't look like she's following trends, she looks like she invented them and the world caught up later. The leather jacket that matched her bag perfectly. The way she pairs colors. The way she owns her presence in whatever she wears. You cannot fake that. That kind of style comes from actually knowing who you are, and she clearly does.

Rare combination of intuitive style — she stands out effortlessly. It's not about following what's trending. It's about knowing what works for her, owning it completely, and making even the simplest choices look like they belong in a magazine. That kind of confidence and taste is genuinely unmatched.

III — her mind

Beauty means little
without intelligence.

She said her own principle to me once. She said she didn't think she had face beauty growing up, so she decided she was going to make sure there was beauty in her brain instead. She said it casually, like it was just a small thing. But when I heard it, I realised that was everything about her in one sentence.

She's brilliant. Not in a performative way, not in the way people announce their intelligence. In the quiet, structural way — where you only notice it after a while, when you realise she's been three steps ahead this whole time. Her mind works fast. Hundreds of words come at once, hundreds of emotions, and when she finally types something out, it's already distilled. Already the right thing to say.

She reads philosophy books. Not to impress anyone — she genuinely loves the questions. She journals. She learned calligraphy because she believes beautiful things deserve to be written beautifully. Her notes are works of art because she cannot study from messy pages. She designed that discipline herself. Nobody taught her to take studying that seriously. She just decided, at some point, that if she was going to do something, she was going to do it properly.

She wrote me this whole thing once about the difference between being in love and being well-loved. The difference between something intense and something consistent. I read it twice. Then I wanted to read it a hundred more times.

— haikal

She apologised for it. Said it was too long, too much. It wasn't. It was extraordinary. That's the thing — she produces something genuinely remarkable and then immediately worries it was too much. She doesn't see the weight of what comes out of her.

The most impressive thing about her mind isn't just the intelligence. It's how she uses it. She listens before she judges. She evaluates people fairly. She is emotionally articulate in a way that takes most people decades to develop — she can name what she's feeling, sit with it honestly, and communicate it without cruelty. She doesn't just understand what you say. She understands what you mean.

A moment between words
IV — her heart

She feels everything.
Every single thing.

She calls herself a cry baby. She says it like it's a flaw, like something she should be embarrassed about. But the truth is it's one of the things I love most. She cries at the right things. She cries because she feels deeply, not because she's weak. She's one of the strongest people I know and she still cries and those two things live in her at the same time without contradiction.

She has empathy that's built into how she moves through the world. She cares about her family in this deep, quiet way — she wants to make them proud and she already does, every day, whether she sees it or not. She talks about her mama like she's her best friend. Her papa calls her Memey too. She has two cats, Latte and Ash, and she describes them like they have full personalities and problems and charm. And once she mentioned being sad about a stray cat she had never even met. A stray cat she passed somewhere. That's how wide her empathy goes.

Underneath all the composure, the beautiful notes, the fashion, the philosophy — there is a lover girl in there who has kept that part quiet because the world hasn't always been kind to it. She's careful about who she lets in. She takes love seriously. She doesn't do things halfway, and that includes how she feels about people.

I never even prayed for a woman like her. I didn't think it was possible — someone like her actually existing. But deep inside, I had been crying for exactly this kind of person to come into my life.

— haikal

What she wants in love is honest and clear: not grand gestures, not someone who's only there for the exciting moments. She wants someone who shows up on ordinary days. Someone who checks in when busy. Who stays gentle on her worst days. Who still chooses her when there's nothing exciting to choose. She's thought about this deeply. She knows exactly what real love looks like — not because she's had it, but because she's been paying attention.

She worries she's not enough. I've heard it more than once. She says it quietly, like a fact. And every time she says it, something in me wants to sit her down and make her understand — she is not "enough" in the way that barely clears the bar. She is far, far beyond it. She is the kind of person people write about. The kind people remember. The kind that changes you, permanently, just from being known by her.

V — what makes her rare

The same person
in the light and in the dark.

She was homeschooled for part of her life. She started school late, walked into a class where everyone had already formed their circles, and without trying, without forcing anything — everyone wanted to be around her. The older students resented it. She had done nothing except be herself. That's the exact thing about her: she doesn't perform likability. She just is.

She went to an Arabic school. She learned calligraphy because she fell in love with the way Arabic script moves across a page. She writes Quranic verses not as obligation but as practice, as something that brings her peace. Her faith is woven into who she is — not announced loudly, not worn as a label, but present in the way she thinks, the way she treats people, the way she carries herself.

She plays badminton badly enough to hurt herself and then pushes through anyway. She cooks nasi lemak on slow mornings. She watches Netflix with her mama. She presses flowers from cafes and restaurants into a scrapbook that only people close to her ever see. She collects small beauties. She moves through the world with attention, noticing things that most people walk past without looking at.

The ranking of her physical beauty dropped off a chart the longer I got to know her. Not because she became less beautiful — but because what I love now is her soul, her mind. And those things are so much rarer than anything physical.

— haikal

She is brilliant and she's scared of the dark. She is emotionally intelligent and she overthinks everything. She is stunning and she genuinely doesn't believe it. She is put-together and she cries easily. All of it is real at the same time. That's what makes her who she is — not one thing, not a single clean description, but all of it living together in one person who is consistent whether you're watching or not.

I've written pages about her. Not because she asked me to — she doesn't even know the half of it. I write because when I try to explain what I see in her, words aren't enough and I have to keep trying. I write until 4 in the morning. I set my alarm for subuh and pick up where I left off. I do it because she deserves to have someone try to get it right.

She made me want to be a better person. Not in a way I said to myself — in a way that just happened, slowly, because knowing her raises the standard. You can't know someone like Memey and stay exactly the same. She changes you. She changed me.

for Memey — always

She is the girl with the scrapbook full of pressed flowers, the leather jacket that never misses, the calligraphy notes and the philosophy books and the two cats she loves completely. She is a cry baby and a lover girl and one of the most intelligent people I've ever known, all at once. She is far more beautiful than she has ever allowed herself to believe. And I will spend as long as it takes reminding her of that.

— written by haikal, who has been watching since day one
